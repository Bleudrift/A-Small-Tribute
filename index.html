What Is #100DaysOfCode?

#100DaysOfCode is a social media challenge that launched on Twitter to help anyone — from newbie to life-long coder — achieve his or her coding goals. The challenge are simple: for 100 days, code everyday for at least one hour and post your progress on Twitter. For more information about the challenge, check out www.100daysofcode.

Why the Tribute?

Learning to code for someone with zero prior experience is not for the faint of heart because initial forays into "coding" can feel like one is diving into the deepest, darkest part of the Mariana Trench — an inscrutable, alien realm.

The learning process itself can be intimidating and overwhelming. The subject matter is a wild, evolving expanse — so many langages to learn and new ones popping up all the time. To apprentice to even one language, one needs intestinal fortitude not simply to survive, but to prevail against endless trials, errors, and failures.

Fortunately, there is a community that supports, encourages, and celebrates the coding novice — #100DaysofCode community on Twitter. Here's why that online group is awesome for anyone (but especially newbies) who are learning to code:

  • Warm, welcoming, supportive community of like-minded individuals;
  • Culture is characterized by continuous learning;
  • Resources galore;
  • Awareness that ALL developers aspire to learn regardless of experience;
  • The guiding principle is to lift up others in the community;
  • Safe space to freely share frustrations, victories, and humor;
  • Suggestions, insights offered with courteous, unpretentious friendliness;
  • Opportunity to develop friendships with others on the same coding path
